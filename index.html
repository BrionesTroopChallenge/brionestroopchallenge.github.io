Cotton is Rotten
7/17/2017

Synthetic clothes are worth every cent!  Cotton works well for everyday life.  It feels great and so is great for shirts.  It absorbs water and so is great for towels.  It looks nice so is great for pants.  But outside of the city, cotton is outperformed.  Synthetic clothes - those from materials like polyester or spandex - dry faster and feel lighter.

So how can you use this to my advantage?  It is true that outside of extreme circumstances, cotton will not kill you.  However, wet cotton can give you a chill you and weigh you down.  I am not just talking about rain.  Sweat can make your shirts and underwear heavier.  Cotton retains more sweat than synthetic alternatives.

From my experience, backpackers with synthetic clothes generally have a better time and less "sweat spots."  When I say "sweat spots," I am referring to those places on your lower back and around your hips where your backpack is touching and is close proximity to your body.

Enough talk about comfort.  What is the big problem with cotton?  In a situation where your clothes are drenched in cold weather or at night, you will want to immediately get on dry clothes and start to gradually warm yourself up.  If all the clothes you have with you are the wet cotton clothes you already have on, then you are in danger.  On the other hand, the odds of survival are much greater for someone that has quick-drying synthetic clothes and clothes that do not weigh you down from lots of absorbed water.

Wear more synthetics.  Stop wearing cotton clothes.  Cotton is rotten.  Not only is it more comfortable in the long run, but it is a good idea and could save your life.

3 Reasons to Pick Up Backpacking This Weekend
7/18/2017



Wilderness backpacking is an amazing, encaptivating sport for people of all ability levels.  Wilderness backpacking brings you to places you have never seen before and places you might never see otherwise.  With your own two feet, you can reach places inacessable by road.


Although backpacking requires a more than a nominal amount of preparations and instruction, a successful backpacking trip can be rewarding. Wilderness backpacking is just that.  Backpacking in the wilderness.  It is a simple concept, but can be very fun and exciting experience. Backpacking is also a great way to get to know your friends better.  Once you become an experienced planner, invite your friends to join you on a weekend trip in the woods.

Backpacking is also a quintessential part of being a Boy Scout.  Anyone who has spent time in any Boy Scout Troop knows the thrill of camping.  More serious Boy Scouts know the relaxation that comes from camping in the middle of nowhere, miles away from civilization and how it feels to be left to their own devices.
